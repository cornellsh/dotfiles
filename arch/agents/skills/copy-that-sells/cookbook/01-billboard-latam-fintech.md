# Example 1: Billboard for a LatAm fintech

A worked example of the skill applied to a Spanish-language OOH brief for a fictional fintech in Buenos Aires.

---

## The brief from the user

> Necesito un billboard para nuestra app de ahorros. Se llama Caja. Vamos a poner 12 billboards en Palermo y Belgrano. Target: jóvenes profesionales de 25 a 35 años en Argentina. La promesa central es que la app guarda automáticamente un porcentaje de cada transacción que hacés con la tarjeta. Sin tener que pensarlo. Tono: dry, confiado, rioplatense.

Inputs extracted in the briefing pass:

- One thing: convince the reader to download the app.
- Reader: 25 to 35 year old professional in Buenos Aires who has tried to save and failed.
- Current belief: "Saving requires discipline I do not have."
- Promise: "Caja saves a slice of every card swipe, automatically."
- Proof to be implied by the visual: an actual saving balance growing on a phone screen.
- Format: 12 OOH placements in Palermo and Belgrano. 3 second read.
- Voice: dry, confident, rioplatense (Voice 1 blended with regional voseo).
- CTA: download the app. QR code on the bottom right.

---

## The output

### Idea
Ahorrar sin querer es ahorrar más.

### Final copy

**Headline (variant A, the production headline)**
> Lo que no pensás, lo ahorrás.

**Headline (variant B, used on the 4 billboards near nightlife)**
> Anoche gastaste. Caja ahorró.

**Headline (variant C, used on the 4 billboards near coworking spaces)**
> Si pudieras ahorrar sin acordarte, ¿lo harías?

**Sub line (optional, only on board sizes that allow it)**
> Caja ahorra un poco de cada compra. Sin que lo pienses.

**CTA**
> Caja. Bajala. (with QR)

### Alternates

1. **Direct**: "Caja ahorra por vos." Plain. Loses the punchline.
2. **Question**: "¿Cuánta plata gastaste este mes sin darte cuenta?" Lands the pain but does not name Caja in the first read.
3. **Reframe**: "El ahorro era un trabajo. Ya no." Generalises away from the act of swiping a card.
4. **Object**: a billboard that shows a real card swipe receipt with a circled line item ("$340 ahorrado en Caja"). Object as headline.

### Notes

Word count of variant A: 5 words. Three second read clean.

Framework doing the work: Bly's Indirect headline category, with a verb-at-the-end rioplatense rhythm. "Lo que no pensás, lo ahorrás" sets up the contrast (pensar vs ahorrar) and lands the verb on the second clause. The voseo ("pensás," "ahorrás") signals local without becoming caricature.

Variants B and C apply the 4 U's differently. B is news adjacent ("Caja ahorró" past tense, factual). C is a provocative question that earns the second look from a reader stopped at a light.

Spanish craft moves applied:
- Drop subject pronouns ("Vos" implied, never written).
- Verb at the end of both clauses to produce the rhythmic landing.
- Cut the article ("el ahorro," "la plata") wherever the sentence still reads.
- Avoid banned vocabulary ("potenciar," "innovador," "robusto," "transformar"). None used.

Self-edit pass: read aloud at a Palermo cafe pace. Five words, three seconds. No em dashes, no hyphens, no compound words. Headline survives without the visual; visual (a phone balance growing) deepens the headline without explaining it.

---

## Postmortem: what the skill did

The briefing pass extracted eight inputs in two minutes. The idea pass produced four candidate ideas; the chosen idea ("Ahorrar sin querer es ahorrar más") was the one closest to the reader's current belief about saving (that it requires effort) and reframed it in a single sentence. The headline pass tested all eight Bly categories; Indirect was the strongest because the curiosity ("¿qué es lo que no pienso?") earned the second read. The Spanish craft pass dropped subject pronouns and verb positions to compress five words further from a 7-word first draft. The CTA pass produced "Bajala" (two syllables in rioplatense, casual imperative) instead of "Descargá la app" (six syllables, formal).

The whole job took roughly 12 minutes of work. The reference files used: `frameworks.md` (Bly's eight + 4 U's), `craft.md` (compression), `formats.md` (billboard blueprint), `spanish-craft.md` (verb position + rioplatense voseo).

What to steal from this example: when writing in Spanish, decide on the variant of Spanish before the first word, drop subject pronouns aggressively, and let the verb fall at the end of one or both clauses. The compression is in the syntax, not the vocabulary.
